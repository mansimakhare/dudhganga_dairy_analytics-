### Project Overview
This project is a comprehensive data analysis of a fictional e-commerce business, Dudhganga Dairy. The goal was to act as a data analyst for the company, moving from raw sales data to actionable business insights. The analysis covers sales performance, customer behavior, and predictive modeling to forecast future trends and identify at-risk customers.

---

### Data Sources
The primary dataset was synthetically generated using a Python script to simulate real-world e-commerce transactions. This includes three main tables:
- **Customers:** Contains unique customer IDs and demographic information.
- **Products:** Contains product IDs, names, categories, and prices.
- **Orders:** Contains transactional data linking customers to the products they've purchased.

### Analytical Questions Answered
1.  What are our best-selling and most profitable products?
2.  What are the key trends in our monthly sales revenue?
3.  Who are our most valuable customers (VIPs)?
4.  Can we identify distinct groups or segments within our customer base?
5.  Can we predict which customers are at high risk of churning?

---

### Feature Engineering
To answer the analytical questions, several new features were engineered from the raw data:
- **Revenue:** Calculated as `quantity * price` for each order line.
- **RFM Metrics (for each customer):**
    - **Recency:** Days since the customer's last purchase.
    - **Frequency:** Total number of orders made by the customer.
    - **Monetary:** Total revenue generated by the customer.
- **Churn Flag (`is_churned`):** A binary flag where `1` indicates a customer is considered churned.

### Key Insights from Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- **Top Selling Product:** The most frequently sold product is **Paneer Cubes (200g)**.
- **Most Profitable Product:** The product generating the most revenue is **Ghee (500g)**.
- **Sales Trend:** The monthly revenue trend shows **a highly seasonal business with strong underlying growth. While sales have grown significantly over the period, there are predictable peaks and volatile dips, suggesting that sales are driven by seasonal factors or specific marketing campaigns**.
- **Top Customer:** Our most valuable customer is **Anay Chand**, who has spent a total of **₹3,705**.

### Machine Learning Implementation

#### 1. Customer Segmentation (K-Means Clustering)
- An unsupervised K-Means Clustering model was built using the RFM metrics to segment customers.
- The "Elbow Method" suggested that the optimal number of clusters was **4**.
- This resulted in the identification of key customer personas such as **"Champions," "At-Risk Customers," and "New Customers,"** allowing for targeted marketing strategies.

#### 2. Churn Prediction (Logistic Regression)
- A supervised Logistic Regression model was trained to predict the likelihood of a customer churning.
- The model achieved an accuracy of **98%** on the unseen test data.
- The model coefficients revealed that **`Recency`** was the strongest predictor of churn.

---

### Conclusions & Recommendations
- **Recommendation 1:** Launch a "VIP Program" for the top customers identified in the analysis to increase loyalty and retention.
- **Recommendation 2:** Create a targeted "Win-Back" email campaign for customers identified by the churn model as being "at-risk."
- **Recommendation 3:** Promote the most profitable products more heavily, even if they are not the most frequently sold.
